Feminist Qur'anic Interpretation – Image Gallery

This gallery offers a visual overview of feminist approaches to the Qur'an. The collection includes images of leading scholars such as Amina Wadud and Asma Barlas, the covers of their most influential books, and symbolic photographs of women that represent themes of interpretation, justice, and equality. It serves as an academic reflection on the intersection of gender and sacred text

Amina Wadud

Amina Wadud is a scholar of Islamic studies and a pioneering voice in Islamic feminism. She is best known for her gender-inclusive interpretation of the Qur'an and her advocacy for the equality of men and women within Islamic texts. Her influential book, "Qur'an and Woman," has played a significant role in rethinking gender in Islam. One of her most controversial acts was leading a mixed-gender Friday prayer, sparking global debates across Muslim communities.

Asma Barlas

Asma Barlas is a prominent Muslim scholar and political scientist known for her work on Islam, gender, and hermeneutics. Her groundbreaking book, Believing Women in Islam: Unreading Patriarchal Interpretations of the Qur'an, challenges traditional patriarchal readings and advocates for a liberating and egalitarian interpretation of the Qur'an. Barlas emphasizes the importance of reading the Qur'an in its historical and linguistic context while rejecting patriarchal authority imposed through secondary texts. Her scholarship has had a major influence on contemporary Islamic feminist thought.
